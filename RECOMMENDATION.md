This lineup wins a 20-opponent pool (21 entries) **9.0%** of the time vs a 4.8% fair share - about 1.9x edge.

**Durable edges (robust to assumptions):**
- **Fade the Tier-2 chalk:** take a strong, less-popular T2 team instead of the odds-on favorite the field piles onto.
- **Soft-path value in T3/T4:** the best EV in the middle tiers comes from teams in weak groups, not the tier's betting favorite.
- Top-tier and bottom-tier picks are close to chalk (the favorite is also the best pick); the leverage lives in the middle.

_Note: the top several portfolios are within Monte-Carlo noise of each other (~0.2%); treat the exact #1 as one of a cluster. The consistent signals above matter more than the precise ranking._

## Pick matrix: pool size x chalkiness (reference)

**How to read it:**
- **Bigger pool -> lower win%** (more competition for one prize), but the *picks* barely move with size alone - they move with chalk.
- **Chalkier field -> fade the crowded favorites harder.** Low chalk: just take the best (EV) team. Medium: fade the Tier-2 favorite. High: fade Tier-1 and Tier-2 favorites toward lower-owned, high-ceiling teams.
- Win% is **U-shaped in chalk**: a scattered field is easy to beat, a chalk-hammering field is exploitable via leverage, and the *typical* pool in between is the hardest to win.
- In the **deep-fade cells** (big + high-chalk), the exact contrarian team is within Monte-Carlo noise - e.g. T1 Argentina <-> Brazil, T2 Netherlands <-> Belgium are interchangeable. The signal is *fade the favorite to a lower-owned elite*, not the precise name.
